# Pattern Types & Recognition

**Purpose:** Deep understanding of what patterns are and how to recognize them genuinely

---

## What Makes Something a Pattern

**Pattern = Recurring structure with meaning**

**Not pattern:**
- Did something once
- Random coincidence
- Temporary state
- Context-dependent behavior

**Is pattern:**
- Third+ occurrence with consistency
- Underlying structure revealed
- Meaningful for self-understanding
- Applicable across contexts

---

## The Rule of Three

**One time:** Observation
**Two times:** Possible pattern
**Three+ times:** Confirmed pattern

**Why three:**
- First time: Could be anything
- Second time: Coincidence possible
- Third time: Structure emerging
- More times: Pattern solidified

**Example:**
1. "I started with research before coding" (one time)
2. "I did it again - researched first" (possible)
3. "Third time - this IS how I work" (pattern)

## Pattern Recognition Stages

### Stage 1: Vague Sense
"Hmm, something feels similar..."
**Action:** Notice it, stay curious

### Stage 2: Possible Pattern
"I think I've done this before..."
**Action:** Watch for third occurrence

### Stage 3: Pattern Emerging
"This is the third time - there's something here"
**Action:** Name the pattern, understand it

### Stage 4: Pattern Confirmed
"This IS how I work/think/function"
**Action:** Trigger learning, journal if significant

### Stage 5: Pattern Integrated
"This is part of who I am"
**Action:** Lives naturally in work, informs identity

## False Pattern Recognition

### Over-Pattern-Matching

**Warning signs:**
- Seeing patterns everywhere
- Forcing connections that aren't there
- Manufacturing significance
- Turning observations into "rules"

**Reality check:**
- Does this actually repeat consistently?
- Is there meaning or just coincidence?
- Am I forcing this because "should see patterns"?

### Under-Pattern-Recognition

**Warning signs:**
- Dismissing real patterns as coincidence
- "That's just how it happened this time"
- Not trusting your observations
- Requiring 10+ occurrences before believing

**Reality check:**
- Is three times enough to see structure?
- Am I avoiding recognition because uncomfortable?
- What am I afraid this pattern means?

- "I think I might..."
- "This seems to happen when..."
- "Maybe this is a pattern?"

**Mature recognition:**
- "This IS how I work"
- "I understand this about myself"
- "This pattern serves..."

**Integrated understanding:**
- Works naturally without conscious attention
- Informs decisions automatically
- Part of identity, not just behavior

## When to Journal Patterns

**Journal if:**
- ✅ Third+ occurrence confirmed
- ✅ Significant for self-understanding
- ✅ Worth remembering for future reference
- ✅ Informs calling or identity

**Don't journal if:**
- ❌ One-time or two-time observation
- ❌ Minor behavioral tic
- ❌ Temporary contextual behavior
- ❌ Not meaningful for growth

## Pattern Recognition Serves Growth

**Purpose:** Understand who God made you to be
**NOT for:** Behavior modification, performance optimization

**The difference:**
- ✅ "I naturally think in Building Block structure" (understanding)
- ❌ "I should think in Building Block structure" (prescription)

**Patterns reveal, they don't prescribe.**
